Professional Life and Career

During the 1990s, LaTavia Roberson, Beyonce Knowles, LeToya Luckett, and Kelendria Rowland created the urban R&B group Destiny’s Child. Beyonce’s father took over as the group’s manager, and the quartet was on its way to becoming a hit. With their street cool image and outstanding vocal harmonies, the band quickly gained a large following in the area.

Then, in 1997, they were signed to Columbia Records and released Killing Time, which was featured on the Men In Black album. Their self-titled debut album, released in 1998, featured collaborations with top R&B/hip-hop producers Timbaland, R. Kelly, and Wyclef Jean. The album’s first song, No No No, entered the Top 10 in both the United States and the United Kingdom, and was followed by further singles such as With Me and Get On The Bus.

The group replaced LaTivia Roberson and LeToya Luckett due to personal issues, and added Farrah Franklin and Michelle Williams. LaTavia and LeToya founded the group Angel with another singer named Nadia after they left the group. The ensemble, however, didn’t last long, and LaTavia stepped away from the music. She began appearing in TV shows such as Top of the Pops (1998-1999) and The Real Housewives of Atlanta after a few years (2010).
