What Happened to Ben Askren?

September 2024 Â· 3 minute read

Ben Askren, a prominent figure in the world of mixed martial arts (MMA), has had a career marked by both successes and setbacks.

From his achievements in wrestling to his transition to MMA and his recent retirement due to a hip injury, Askren's journey has been filled with noteworthy experiences that have left a lasting impact on the sport.

A Successful Career in Wrestling and Transition to MMA

Ben Askren's journey in combat sports began with a successful wrestling career. From high school state titles to junior national titles to becoming a four-time All-American wrestler, Askren's prowess in wrestling laid the foundation for his transition to MMA.

His accolades extended to the international stage, with accomplishments such as world teams and national titles, culminating in his participation as a US Olympian.

In MMA, Askren carved out a niche for himself, becoming a former Bellator and One Championship title-holder before making his much-anticipated debut in the UFC in March.

Despite a previous retirement in 2017, Askren's return to the sport was met with high expectations due to his undefeated record at the time and his success outside the UFC.

Ups and Downs in the UFC

Askren's stint in the UFC was characterized by a mix of triumphs and challenges.

He secured a controversial victory over former welterweight champion Robbie Lawler before facing setbacks in his subsequent fights, including a record-breaking five-second knockout loss to Jorge Masvidal and a submission loss to Demian Maia.

Despite these losses, Askren's impact on the sport was evident as he garnered respect from fellow fighters and fans alike for his contributions and the entertainment value he brought to each of his fights.

Retirement Due to a Hip Injury

The most recent and significant development in Ben Askren's career is his retirement from MMA due to a hip injury.

Askren revealed that he had been grappling with hip issues for several years, and after seeking medical advice and undergoing an MRI, it became apparent that a hip replacement was necessary, effectively marking the end of his MMA career.

The impact of the hip injury on Askren's decision to retire was profound, with the realization that the injury would significantly impede his ability to continue competing in MMA.

Despite the challenges he faced, Askren expressed gratitude for the opportunities he had and the successes he achieved throughout his career.

Analysis of Career Trajectory

Ben Askren's career trajectory reflects the multifaceted nature of professional sports, encompassing triumphs, setbacks, and the physical toll that competitive athletics can take on an individual.

His journey from a decorated wrestler to a prominent MMA figure showcased his adaptability and determination to excel in different arenas of combat sports.

The successes and setbacks in Askren's MMA career serve as a testament to the unpredictable nature of professional fighting, where even seasoned athletes like him can face unexpected challenges.

The impact of injuries, such as the hip issues that ultimately led to his retirement, underscores the physical demands and risks associated with combat sports at the highest level.

Despite the challenges he encountered in the UFC, Askren's influence on the sport and the respect he garnered from his peers highlight the lasting legacy he leaves behind.

His ability to transition from wrestling to MMA, secure titles in different organizations, and captivate audiences with his performances contributed to his status as a significant figure in the sport.

 

All in all, Ben Askren's career is a testament to the resilience and determination required to succeed in the world of combat sports.

His journey, marked by triumphs and setbacks, culminated in a retirement prompted by a debilitating hip injury. But Askren's impact on MMA transcends his retirement, as his contributions and the entertainment he provided in the octagon have left an indelible mark on the sport.
