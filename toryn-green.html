Biography

Toryn Green was born in Houston, TX on November 3, 1975. Vocalist is known for his work with a variety of American rock bands, including Fuel, Emphatic, and Apocalyptica. He made his acting debut playing Beckett in the action sci-fi film Skate God. He replaced Brett Scallions as the lead singer for Fuel in 2007. He graduated from Colony High School in Palmer, Alaska in 1994. His first public performance was at the Alaska State Fair.

Career

A rock singer and actor from the United States. He used to be the lead singer for the hard rock band Fuel, as well as Emphatic and For The Taking. He was also the touring lead vocalist for Apocalyptica, performing on both of the band’s 2008 Worlds Collide Tours in the United States.

Green has recorded and released two full-length studio albums with Fuel and Emphatic: Angels & Devils (Epic, 2007) and Another Life (Epochal Artists, 2013).
