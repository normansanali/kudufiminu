How Often Does The Favorite Win The Super Bowl?

The favorite won the Super Bowl only twice, in 2018 and 2019. New England Patriots and Kansas City Chiefs won the big games, respectively.

For 3 years, in 2019, 2020, and 2021, the Kansas City Chiefs were the team with the largest bet. They emerged victorious in 2019 in a match against the San Francisco 49ers held at Mercedes-Benz Stadium in Atlanta.

The Chiefs lost to Tampa Bay and L.A. Rams in the subsequent years. But they have always been one of the favorites. The Chiefs fan were elated when their most loved football squad won Super Bowl 57.

The championship game of the NFL is a big day of celebration. Viewers from across the globe tune into the broadcast. But what makes it even more interesting is betting and rooting for your favorite squad.

It adds to the thrill of the game. As per CNBC, Super Bowl LVII saw an increase of 25% more in betting than the previous years. Looking at the records at Buffalo News, it is clear that except for two times, the favorites have always lost.

    • Recent Back To Back Winner

    Last back to back Super Bowl winners was New England Patriots. The team consecutively won Super Bowls XXXVIII and XXXIX in 2004 and 2005.

    On 1 February 2004, they won against Carolina Panthers with a 32-29 score. The coveted match was held at Reliant Stadium in Houston, Texas.

    The following year in 2005, the annual final playoff game of the NFL happened at Alltel Stadium, Jacksonville, Florida. In the final showdown, New England Patriots gained victory over Philadelphia Eagles with the score of 24-21, according to ESPN.

    It could have been their 3rd win in a row if they had reached the finals in the 2006 Super Bowl. But it was the time for Pittsburgh Steelers to shine through that year.
