Yellowstone invites you to spend Memorial Day Weekend with the Duttons

September 2024 · 2 minute read

Calling all Dutton family fans, the Paramount Network invites you to spend Memorial Day weekend with your favorite ranching family with a Yellowstone marathon. Starting this Friday, you’ll be able to begin a new journey through every episode of Yellowstone and all of the chaos and love that comes with them. 

Recommended Videos

That’s right, as you honor those who laid their lives on the line for us at various gatherings over the weekend, Yellowstone will serve as the best television series to have your televisions tuned to. With a family dedicated to doing what’s right, fighting for one another and their family, and hell-bent on destroying the enemy — Paramount knows what they’re doing in giving fans a weekend with the Duttons.

Audiences tune into Yellowstone for a multitude of reasons; from the fights and the drama to the sensuality and romance — there’s something for every facet of entertainment fan within the episodes we’ve grown to know and love. I can’t tell you how many times I’ve re-watched the Dutton family story, but I can tell you that there’s something about the series that bridges a gap. It brings people from different classes and social statuses together, uniting friends and family with the kind of story that brings us together for laughs, tears, and a lot of awe-inspiring moments.

Kicking off Friday at 4 p.m. on Paramount, you’ll see every episode of Yellowstone so far across the long weekend. That means recapping every romantic moment that swept us off of our feet, reliving every fight and trip to the train station, and examining the exact place we left the Dutton family before the heart of the series became uprooted, and fans learned that we’ll soon take our final ride with them as the characters they are now.

So as you’re packing your coolers and bags to ring in the weekend, we suggest a pack of tissues, too; your Yellowstone-loving friends will be more emotional than ever as they watch Kevin Costner‘s John Dutton set the standard for who men are in the Yellowverse and what level of intensity, passion, and die-hard stubbornness they must exist with to carry on the family name.
